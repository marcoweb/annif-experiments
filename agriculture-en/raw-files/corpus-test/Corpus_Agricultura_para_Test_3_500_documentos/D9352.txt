#ITI#Root system responses to phosphate nutrition involve plasma membrane H+-ATPases in Arabidopsis thaliana#FTI#
#IRE#Phosphate deficiency is one of the primary abiotic stresses that causes serious problems in crop growth and productivity. Plant roots are the first organs that perceive a signal of nutrient shortage and excess. Plant plasma membrane H+-ATPases are proton pumps that play a central role in almost all physiological processes, such as nutrient uptake, pH regulation, stomatal opening, cell growth and adaptation of plants to stress conditions. It has not yet been clarified whether these proteins are required for root growth regulation under different Pi regimes. Therefore, this study aimed to analyse the expression of plasma membrane H+-ATPase genes (AHA1, AHA2, AHA7) in Arabidopsis thaliana roots in response to different phosphate nutrition (starvation and high level). Moreover, the effect of deletions in these three members of the proton pump family on root system architecture as well as the involvement of the SAUR19 gene was investigated in more detail. These studies indicate that AHA2 might control pH homeostasis in the process of root growth and together with AHA7 seem to participate in the regulation of root elongation of primary and lateral roots in response to different Pi supply. © 2022, The Author(s) under exclusive licence to Franciszek Górski Institute of Plant Physiology, Polish Academy of Sciences, Kraków.#FRE#
