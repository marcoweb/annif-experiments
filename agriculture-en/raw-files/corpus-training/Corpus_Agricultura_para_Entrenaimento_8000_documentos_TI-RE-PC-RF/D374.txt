#ITI#CaRH57, a RNA helicase, contributes pepper tolerance to heat stress#FTI#
#IRE#RNA helicases (RHs) are required for most aspects of RNA metabolism and play an important role in plant stress tolerance. Heat stress (HS) causes the deleterious effects on plant cells, such as membrane disruption and protein misfolding, which results in the inhibition of plant growth and development. In this study, CaRH57 was identified from pepper (Capsicum annuum) and encodes a DEAD-box RH. CaRH57 was induced by HS, and overexpression of CaRH57 in Atrh57-1 rescued the glucose-sensitive phenotype of Atrh57-1, suggesting the functional replacement of CaRH57 to AtRH57. The nucleolus-localized CaRH57 possessed a RH activity in vitro. CaRH57 knockdown impaired pepper heat tolerance, showing severe necrosis and enhanced ROS accumulation in the region of the shoot tip. Additionally, accumulation of aberrant-spliced CaHSFA1d and CaHSFA9d was enhanced, and the corresponding mature mRNA levels were reduced in the TRV2 (Tobacco rattle virus)-CaRH57-infected plants compared with the control plants under HS. Overall, these results suggested that CaRH57 acted as a RH to confer pepper heat tolerance and was required for the proper pre-mRNA splicing of some HS-related genes. © 2023 Elsevier Masson SAS#FRE#
