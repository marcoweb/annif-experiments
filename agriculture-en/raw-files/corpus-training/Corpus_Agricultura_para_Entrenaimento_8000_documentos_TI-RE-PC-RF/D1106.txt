#ITI#Protein elicitor GP1pro targets aquaporin NbPIP2;4 to activate plant immunity#FTI#
#IRE#The novel protein elicitor GP1pro is the protein component of glycoprotein GP-1 isolated and identified from Streptomyces kanasensis ZX01. GP1pro induces the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and a hypersensitive response (HR), along with the accumulation of resistance-related genes and secondary metabolites. It ultimately regulates plant defence responses. Further analysis revealed that GP1pro interacts with the PIP2-family aquaporin protein NbPIP2;4 on the plant plasma membrane (PM) in Nicotiana benthamiana. PM localization is necessary for inducing GP1pro resistance. These results demonstrate that NbPIP2;4 acts as a H2O2 transporter to positively regulate plant immunity and ROS accumulation. In summary, this study elucidates a conserved and novel pathway caused by GP1pro to initiate host cellular defences by targeting the plant aquaporin protein NbPIP2;4 and transporting apoplast-to-cytoplast H2O2 to regulate plant immunity. © 2023 John Wiley & Sons Ltd.#FRE#
