#ITI#A molecular framework for understanding the phylogeny of spiranthes (Orchidaceae), A cosmopolitan genus with a North American center of diversity#FTI#
#IRE#Premise of the study: Spiranthes is a genus of small terrestrial orchids that are most diverse in North America, yet unusually cosmopolitan, including many of conservation concern. Taxonomy based on morphology alone is problematic, but molecular evidence could help resolve evolutionary relationships within the genus. The phylogeny of Spiranthes was reconstructed to evaluate these patterns, particularly among North American and Old World lineages, determine the systematic value of chromosome numbers AND phenology, consider aspects of historical biogeography, and provide evidence for the taxonomic status of vulnerable species.#FRE#
